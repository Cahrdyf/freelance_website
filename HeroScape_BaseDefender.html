Heroscape Base Defender

Objective:

Destroy the other player(s) base/ancient!

Winner: Player with the last ancient or army standing.
Loser(s): Players who have lost their ancient/had them destroyed by the other players.


THE ANCIENT


The ancient is a special 7-space tile that the enemy players must attack.

To completely destroy an ancient, an enemy was attack and destroy the numbered "outer" tiles/nodes. (Any order)

An enemy cannot destroy the "The Core" (The "X" tile) until all outer tiles/nodes are destroyed.

Important information for setting up your ancient:

  1. Must be reachable by all sides; at least have one tile space bordering the ancient.
  2. Ancients must be equidistant from each other; on opposite ends of the map.
  3. The path to each must to be reachable by heroes without flying ability, both back and forth. (No one way cliffs/pits, etc.)

ANCIENT STATS (Per Node/Tile)

  • Hitpoints: 1/1 (Since there are 7 nodes, think 7 total hitpoints.)
  • Move: 0
  • Attack: 0 (Cannot attack!)
  • Defense: 3
  • Magic Immunity: The ancients are immune to ability damage. (Dragon's fire breath, grenades, etc.)

Glyphs

Glyphs are possessable items in this gamemode. You will permanently gain the bonuses when one of your heroes picks it up, but only each player can only have ONE glyph at a time. If a player wishes to pick up one they like better, their current one is either:
a.) destroyed and discarded from play or
b.) Dropped in the place where they picked it up.

They keep the new glyph and take the new bonuses and lose the old ones.

After taken, players can Glyphs can be dropped/abandoned on any tile; a player can also destroy glyphs for free/without combat if it is in their possession or they pick it up/land on it to "deny" it for other players.

What does the Glyph of Brandar do?

The Glyph of Brandar can only be obtained after slaying "The Champion" (click me to jump to section) Upon death, the glyph is dropped where the champion once stood. (WARNING: A glyph is NOT picked up until a player's unit lands on it. Thus, they can be "stolen" from the slayer!

After landing on the glyph and deciding what to do with a previously owned glyph (if you had one), Roll the 20-sided die.
Do the following based on the result [for results higher than 10, minus 10. For example 17 would be 7, 20 would be 10, 11 would be 1, etc.]:




1 = Immortality: The gods give you another chance. Bring back one dead hero, giving them full health and refreshing abilities. If you choose to pick a squad, bring all of them back. For squads with survivors, bring all units back and remove all damage from all units. (Cannot be used if all members are alive.)



2 = Dragon's Bond: The gods offer you the restless soul of powerful dragon's ancestor. Give to a hero or a single member of a squad; keep the token underneath the chosen unit. That unit gains all of Mimring's abilities. When unit dies or is removed from play, discard Dragon's Bond from play. Inherits Mimring's movement speed if it is greater than the units base value. If the unit you chose was Mimring, nothing happens and the glyph is destroyed. [MUST BE USED IMMEDIATELY. DESTROY OTHERWISE]



3 = Wrath of Heaven: The gods smite your enemies. Use to deal up to 5 points of damage to any of the enemy player units on the map. Can be spread out to different players. (Example: 2 to one of Player A's heroes and 3 to one of Player B's Hero). Cannot be dealt on heroes who are on their last hitpoint. Squad units may be killed by this effect. Discard after use.



4 = Vitality: The gods heal you. Use to remove up to 5 hitpoints from any of your units.(Discard after use.)



5 = Glimpse: Strange energies surround the Champion's house. Re-roll!



6 = Candy Bar: The gods do not respond, so you just loot the Champion's house. You find a candy bar underneath a piece of furniture. It looks very old but the packaging is still sealed! Use to heal 1 hitpoint from any of your units. (Discard after use.)



7 = Rune of Clairvoyance: The gods do not respond, so you just loot the Champion's house. You find an odd glowing crystal shaped like an eye. Use anytime during your turn to reveal all enemy players' order markers. Discard after use.



8 = Diamond Splitter: The gods do not respond, so you just loot the Champion's house. You find an enchanted maul capable of tearing down castles. Give to one hero or unit and place it under them. For each non-ranged attack on an ancient, roll a 20-sided die. If you roll a 1 or 10 (or any number in between) you find a weak point and attack the core directly regardless of undamaged surrounding nodes. Roll defense dice for ancient like normal.



9 = Masamune: The gods do not respond, so you just loot the Champion's house. You find a katana (japanese sword) possessed by the ghost of a legendary samurai. Give to one hero or unit and place it under them. They gain the "counter-strike" ability (See existing ability for samurai units.) Does nothing and is destroyed immediately if given to a unit that already has this special. When possessing unit dies, discard the Masamune from play.



10 = Rusty Sword: The gods do not respond, so you just loot the Champion's house. You find an old, plain longsword they probably kept for sentimental purposes. Give to one hero or unit and place it under them. They get +1 attack dice in non-ranged combat. When possessing unit dies, discard the Rusty Sword from play.

The Champion

A warrior of great power and military prowess that's seen many battles in their extraordinary life. The gods favor them and rumors say they've collected many rare artifacts in their numerous adventures.

Choosing a champ

Before choosing heroes and units for their army, the players must vote on a hero (no squads) to be the Champ. Note that this hero will be not be available if chosen as the Champ. After potential champs are chosen, each player rolls the 20-sided die. Whoever rolls the highest number has their selected hero become the champ for this game.

Setting up the champ

The champ is a formidable opponent.
Apply the following to the champ:

  1. +3 move for each participating player
  2. +2 hitpoints for each participating player
  3. +1 attack dice for each participating player
  4. +1 defense dice for each participating player
  5. Pre-existing abilities are the same.
  6. Place Glyph of Brandar under champ's unit.

Champ's Lair

The champ starts off in their "lair". Place a 7-tile on the map as equidistant as possible from all the players (the center of the map).

The Champ's Behavior

All order markers will alway be placed on the champ's card since it is a single unit. They are dormant until someone attacks them. When attacked they become active and will move toward and attack the unit that attacked them. (Mark the champs target with a token.) They will keep attacking/pursuing that unit until they are slain or attacked by another unit. The new attacking unit gets targeted, so the target token moves to that unit.

The champ will take the shortest path possible to the attacking unit. The Glyph of Brandar always follows the champ, kept below the unit. When slain, the champ leaves the glyph behind; the next unit to land on the glyph (even if it's not the slayer's) may take the glyph or just destroy and deny it.

If the champ has destroyed their target, they will return back to their lair (taking the shortest path possible) and become dormant. Any unit that attacks them, however, will become the new target.

The champ always ignores and can't use Glyphs.

Ranged Attacks on an Ancient

Facing Walls

Assume that the ancients are rather tall structures. Ranged units can only attack the the tiles/nodes that are facing them. They cannot shoot through them to get at a node in the back.

Ranged Attack Resistance

Nodes get +1 defense die against all ranged attacks. Cores get +2 defense dice against all ranged attacks.
